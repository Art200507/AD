MENTAL HEALTH RESOURCES IN INDIA — FREE AND LOW COST
======================================================

TYPES OF MENTAL HEALTH PROFESSIONALS
--------------------------------------

Psychiatrist:
- A medical doctor (MBBS + MD/DNB Psychiatry) who can diagnose and prescribe medication
- Best for: formal diagnosis, medication management, severe symptoms
- Found at: government hospitals, private clinics, medical colleges

Clinical Psychologist:
- Has an MPhil or PhD in clinical psychology — look for RCI registration
- Provides therapy and psychological assessment — cannot prescribe medication
- Best for: therapy, ADHD assessment, emotional processing, long-term support

Counsellor / Therapist:
- Training varies widely — always ask about qualifications
- Provides talk therapy and emotional support
- Best for: regular emotional support, mild to moderate concerns

GOVERNMENT AND FREE OPTIONS IN INDIA
--------------------------------------

Government Psychiatric OPDs:
- Available at most district hospitals and medical college hospitals across India
- Cost: typically free to ₹50 per visit — one of the most affordable options
- Services include consultation, diagnosis, medication, and basic therapy
- You can usually walk in during OPD hours (typically 9am to 1pm weekdays)
- Wait times can be long, but this is a real, accessible option

NIMHANS (National Institute of Mental Health and Neurosciences), Bangalore:
- India's premier mental health institution
- Provides subsidised outpatient and inpatient services
- Has specific ADHD, child psychiatry, and adult psychiatry clinics
- Tele-consultation services are available

iCall (TISS, Mumbai):
- Run by the Tata Institute of Social Sciences
- Offers individual counselling on a sliding scale — pay what you can afford
- Trained psychologists and counsellors
- Available online and in-person; languages include Hindi and English

District Mental Health Programme (DMHP):
- Government programme running in many districts across India
- Provides free community mental health services
- Available at government hospitals and Community Health Centres
- Medications often provided free under government schemes

AFFORDABLE ONLINE OPTIONS
---------------------------

Several online therapy platforms in India offer sessions in a range from free to affordable:
- Some platforms offer sliding scale fees based on income
- Always verify that your therapist or psychologist has an RCI license
- Video sessions tend to be cheaper than in-person
- University counselling centres are free for enrolled students

iCall offers extended counselling on a sliding scale based on income — worth asking about affordability directly when you reach out.

ADHD-SPECIFIC SUPPORT IN INDIA
--------------------------------

ADHD Diagnosis in India:
- A psychiatrist or clinical psychologist can assess for ADHD
- Assessment involves clinical interview, standardised rating scales, and detailed history
- Formal diagnosis is needed before any medication can be prescribed

ADHD Medication in India:
- Methylphenidate (brand names include Ritalin, Concerta) — requires a Schedule X prescription
- Atomoxetine (brand names include Axetra, Attentrol) — non-stimulant, easier to access
- Not all pharmacies stock these — larger city or hospital pharmacies are better
- Medication alone is not enough: therapy, structure, and self-understanding are essential

Online ADHD Communities (Free Peer Support):
- ADHD Support Group India — active Facebook community of adults and parents
- r/ADHD on Reddit — large international community with many Indian members
- r/IndianMentalHealth — India-specific mental health community, growing and supportive
- These are for peer support, not professional advice — but can be validating and helpful

Free Self-Help for ADHD:
- Body doubling: working alongside someone else (in person or via video)
- External systems: written lists, phone alarms, visible calendars on walls
- Free CBT and DBT worksheets available online — search "free DBT worksheets PDF"
- YouTube channels by ADHD coaches and researchers offer free psychoeducation
- This app is available to you anytime for emotional support

HOW TO FIND PROFESSIONAL HELP ON A TIGHT BUDGET
-------------------------------------------------

Option 1 (Cheapest): Government Hospital Psychiatry OPD
- Walk in during OPD hours
- Registration fee: ₹10 to ₹50, consultation is free or very low cost
- Medications often available for free at the hospital pharmacy

Option 2: Medical College Psychiatry Department
- Psychiatry departments at medical colleges charge ₹50 to ₹200
- Quality is often very good — supervised by experienced faculty
- Sometimes shorter waits than district hospitals

Option 3: iCall Sliding Scale Counselling
- Contact iCall and discuss your financial situation honestly
- They can also refer you to low-cost therapists and psychiatrists in your city

Option 4: NIMHANS Tele-Consultation
- Free, requires an appointment

FINANCIAL SCHEMES THAT MAY HELP
---------------------------------
- PMJAY (Ayushman Bharat): covers hospitalisation including mental health admissions in empanelled hospitals
- Some state governments have additional mental health schemes
- NGOs sometimes offer free counselling — worth researching in your city
- Ask government hospitals directly about any free or subsidised programmes

NOTE ON STIGMA
---------------
Seeking mental health support in India takes real courage. Many families do not understand. "Log kya kahenge" is a real weight to carry. But your mental health matters more than what others think.

You are not weak for struggling. You are not "pagal" for needing help. You are someone managing real challenges, and support exists for you — even when it is hard to reach.

You deserve care. Keep reaching out.
