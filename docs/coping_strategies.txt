COPING STRATEGIES — FREE TOOLS THAT ACTUALLY HELP
===================================================

These strategies cost nothing. They are research-backed and ADHD-aware.
Start with ONE. Not all of them. Just one.

FREE ADHD COPING STRATEGIES
-----------------------------

BODY DOUBLING
What it is: Working in the presence of another person (physically or virtually).
How to use it (free options):
- Study/work at a library or café
- Use FocusMate.com (free tier — virtual body doubling)
- Ask a friend to video call while you both work silently
- Join "study with me" YouTube streams

POMODORO TECHNIQUE
What it is: Work for 25 minutes, break for 5 minutes. Repeat.
Steps:
1. Set a timer for 25 minutes
2. Work on ONE task only
3. When timer rings, stop — even mid-flow
4. Take a 5-minute genuine break (walk, water, stretch)
5. After 4 rounds, take a 20-30 minute break
Why it helps ADHD: Short sprints feel manageable. Timer externalizes time.
Free apps: Forest app (free version) or your phone timer.

EXTERNAL REMINDERS AND SYSTEMS
The ADHD brain cannot rely on memory alone. This is not weakness — it is neuroscience.
Free tools:
- Phone alarms with descriptive labels ("Leave NOW," "Call mum back")
- Sticky notes on mirrors, doors, laptop
- WhatsApp self-message for instant capture
- Google Keep for quick notes
- One dedicated notebook for the most critical tasks
Rule: "If it's not written down, it doesn't exist."

BODY-BASED GROUNDING — 5-4-3-2-1
Use when overwhelmed, panicking, or emotionally flooded.
5 — Name 5 things you can SEE right now
4 — Name 4 things you can TOUCH (actually touch them)
3 — Name 3 things you can HEAR
2 — Name 2 things you can SMELL
1 — Name 1 thing you can TASTE
Why it works: Anxiety lives in past/future. This pulls your brain to NOW.

EMOTIONAL REGULATION — DBT SKILLS
------------------------------------

TIPP SKILL (for intense emotional moments):
T — Temperature: Splash cold water on your face or hold ice. Physically slows your nervous system.
I — Intense exercise: 20 jumping jacks or run on the spot. Burns off emotional flooding.
P — Paced breathing: Inhale 4 counts, hold 4, exhale 6-8. The long exhale calms the nervous system.
P — Paired muscle relaxation: Tense a muscle group 5 seconds, release, notice the release.

OPPOSITE ACTION
When emotion pushes you toward something harmful, do the opposite:
- Shame says "hide" → Reach out to one safe person
- Anxiety says "avoid the task" → Do just 5 minutes of it
- Anger says "send that harsh message" → Leave the room first, wait 20 minutes
This doesn't suppress feelings. It stops them from driving decisions.

RADICAL ACCEPTANCE
Accepting reality as it is — not as you wish it were.
It does NOT mean approving of what happened.
When to use it:
- When you are in pain over something unchangeable
- When fighting reality is making things worse
Mantra: "I don't like this. AND this is what is."

CRISIS COPING TOOLKIT
-----------------------
When in crisis, complex strategies fail. Use SIMPLE:
1. BREATHE — 4 in, 6-8 out. Repeat 5 times.
2. COLD WATER — Splash face, hold an ice cube.
3. NAME IT — "I am feeling _____ right now." Naming reduces intensity.
4. REACH OUT — Text ONE safe person. You don't have to explain everything.
5. DELAY — No major decisions for 24 hours. Crisis + ADHD = regrettable impulsive decisions.
6. HELPLINE — iCall: 9152987821 | Vandrevala: 1860-2662-345 (24/7, free)

SLEEP HYGIENE FOR ADHD
-------------------------
ADHD disrupts sleep. Poor sleep worsens every ADHD symptom.
Why ADHD ruins sleep:
- Brain can't "switch off" at night
- Hyperfocus keeps you up for hours
- Racing thoughts and anxiety at night
- Irregular schedules due to time blindness

Free strategies:
- Phone alarm for "start winding down" — not just for morning
- Stop screens 30-45 minutes before sleep
- Same sleep and wake time daily — even weekends
- Keep room dark and cool
- If you can't sleep: don't scroll in bed. Get up, do something quiet, return when sleepy.

LOW-COST / NO-COST SELF-CARE
------------------------------
MOVEMENT (free):
- Walk 20 minutes daily — the single best free intervention for ADHD and mood
- Stretch when you wake up (5 minutes)
- Dance in your room

SUNLIGHT (free):
- 10-15 minutes morning sunlight regulates mood and sleep
- Sit near a window or step outside

WATER (almost free):
- Dehydration worsens brain fog. Drink water when you wake up.

CONNECTION (free):
- Text someone: "Hey, thinking of you"
- r/ADHD and r/IndianMentalHealth on Reddit (anonymous, supportive)
- iCall free online chat: icallhelpline.org

CREATIVITY (free):
- Write without editing or judging
- Draw or doodle — doesn't have to be good
- Listen to music that matches or shifts your mood

NATURE (free):
- 20 minutes in a park reduces cortisol (stress hormone). Even looking at trees helps.

REMEMBER: Self-care when you have ADHD and limited energy is not selfish. It is maintenance. A phone at 2% battery cannot help anyone. You need charging too.
